MUN Committees

UNSC

The UNSC (United Nations Security Council) is a primary organ of the United Nations responsible for maintaining international peace and security. It consists of 15 member states, with five permanent members (China, France, Russia, UK, US) possessing veto power. The Council issues resolutions, ... authorizes peacekeeping operations, and imposes sanctions to address conflicts and threats to global peace. While it has played a crucial role in addressing international crises, it faces criticisms due to the potential gridlock caused by the P5's veto power. Efforts for reform continue to enhance the UNSC's effectiveness and responsiveness to evolving global challenges.

DISEC

DISEC (Disarmament and International Security Committee), also known as the First Committee of the UN General Assembly, is one of the six main committees of the United Nations. Its primary focus is on disarmament, arms regulation, and international security issues. DISEC addresses challenges related to the proliferation... of weapons of mass destruction, conventional arms control, and disarmament initiatives. It is a forum for member states to deliberate on security concerns, promote confidence-building measures, and draft resolutions aimed at fostering global peace and security. DISEC plays a vital role in shaping international responses to pressing security matters on the UN's agenda.

UNHRC

The UNHRC (United Nations Human Rights Council) is a subsidiary body of the United Nations responsible for addressing human rights issues worldwide. It is composed of 47 member states elected by the UN General Assembly. The UNHRC monitors and investigates human rights violations, holds ... discussions on emerging human rights challenges, and formulates recommendations for action. It plays a critical role in promoting and protecting human rights, supporting vulnerable populations, and addressing humanitarian crises. Through its work, the UNHRC strives to foster dialogue, accountability, and international cooperation to uphold human rights principles and standards globally.

ECOSOC

ECOSOC (United Nations Economic and Social Council) is one of the principal organs of the United Nations. Composed of 54 member states, it serves as a central platform for discussing international economic, social, and environmental issues. ECOSOC plays a key role in promoting sustainable development,... coordinating the work of specialized agencies, and fostering international cooperation to address global challenges. It formulates policy recommendations, initiates studies, and organizes forums to facilitate dialogue and collaboration among governments, NGOs, and other stakeholders. Through its efforts, ECOSOC aims to advance social progress, alleviate poverty, and achieve a more equitable and prosperous world for all.
